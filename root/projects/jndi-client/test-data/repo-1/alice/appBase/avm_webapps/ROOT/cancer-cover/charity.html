To help fight the big ‘C’ we’ve teamed up with one of the UK’s leading cancer campaigns, Everyman, to help increase awareness of male cancer and raise funds for vital research.

RUB SHOULDERS WITH THE STARS AND RAISE MONEY

Fancy a bit of celebrity-spotting, maybe get on the telly or in the Guinness Book of Records? Have a blast and feel saintly at the same time. Check out the star-studded ways we’re raising money for Everyman on our Big V Events pages.

Plus there’s a whole host of other ways to help raise awareness and funds on Everyman’s website – from jumping out of a plane to running a marathon.


WHAT WE’RE RAISING MONEY FOR

The Everyman Campaign was set up by The Institute of Cancer Research to raise awareness of testicular and prostate cancer and to fund vital research within Europe's first and only dedicated male cancer research centre.

Testicular cancer is the most common form of cancer in men aged 15-45 and is now 99% curable if caught early enough. Prostate cancer is the UK's most common cancer in men, affecting more than 30,000 each year.

The incidence of male cancer is rising dramatically, yet awareness is low and research into this area has been severely under-funded. Male cancer doesn’t just affect men, it also affects their partners, mothers, daughters and sisters, so Everyman is big on educating women as well.

To find out more about the Everyman campaign go to www.icr.ac.uk/everyman or call 0800 731 9468.

And for general cancer information, visit The Institute of Cancer Research’s site at www.icr.ac.uk/cancer_info/index.shtml
